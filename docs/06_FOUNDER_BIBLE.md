## Purpose

This document preserves the founder intent behind Market Pulse AI. It is not a technical specification alone. It is the product conscience.

## Why Market Pulse AI Exists

Market Pulse AI exists because investors need a calmer way to understand markets.

Many financial interfaces either overwhelm users with raw numbers or over-simplify decisions into confident signals. Market Pulse AI chooses a third path: show evidence, explain uncertainty, and help the user think.

The product should feel like a disciplined research partner, not a hype machine.

## Problems It Solves

Market Pulse AI helps with:

- scattered market information
- unclear data provenance
- confusing cross-asset comparison
- AI outputs that sound too confident
- missing provider data hidden behind polished UI
- Thai investors needing bilingual context
- users needing watchlists and simulated portfolio awareness without broker risk

## Founder Principles

1. Zero Mock.
2. Never fabricate investment evidence.
3. Always distinguish unavailable data.
4. Professional over flashy.
5. Transparency over hype.
6. User decides.
7. AI explains, not commands.
8. Thai-first does not mean Thai-only.
9. Optional features must not block core research.
10. Release quality matters more than scope.

## Non-Negotiable Rules

Zero Mock:

- no fake prices
- no fake charts
- no fake headlines
- no fake economic events
- no fake sentiment scores
- no fake AI evidence

Investment safety:

- no guaranteed language
- no sure-profit claims
- no instruction that a user must buy or sell
- no hidden limitations

Data transparency:

- show source
- show timestamp where available
- show unavailable reason where available
- preserve nulls when data is missing

## Product Voice

The voice should be:

- direct
- calm
- cautious
- educational
- practical
- bilingual where useful

It should not be:

- promotional
- sensational
- overconfident
- vague
- dismissive of risk

## Investment Language Policy

Allowed cautious wording:

- watch closely
- wait for setup
- high risk
- suitable only for users who can tolerate risk
- define a risk plan before investing
- monitor evidence

Disallowed wording:

- guaranteed
- certain
- must buy
- sure profit
- risk free
- cannot lose

The user must remain responsible for decisions.

## Design Intent

Market Pulse AI should feel like a professional investment terminal:

- dense but readable
- dark but high contrast
- data-first
- stable across refreshes
- compact disclosures
- visible source and risk information

The product should not become a marketing page inside the dashboard.

## Current State

The current release candidate includes a Founder-accepted UI freeze, real-data provider policy, modular backend, frontend dashboard, simulated portfolio, AI committee surfaces, and deployment documentation.

## Future Work

Future work must preserve the principles before adding scope.

Good future work:

- better provider reliability
- clearer evidence trails
- better Thai financial language
- stronger accessibility
- documented premium alerts
- auditable portfolio intelligence

Bad future work:

- fake data to make the UI look complete
- AI signals without evidence
- visual redesign during release freeze
- hidden provider failures

## Known Limitations

- Not a broker.
- Not a licensed adviser.
- Not a substitute for independent research.
- Provider availability can change.
- Analysis is educational and incomplete.

## Version 2.x Vision

Version 2.x should deepen personal intelligence:

- stronger portfolio coach
- alert monitoring with consent
- better provider routing
- more complete fundamentals
- improved Thai summaries
- release automation

## Version 3.x Vision

Version 3.x may expand toward:

- collaborative research
- adviser workflows
- institutional audit trails
- advanced scenario analysis
- deeper risk governance

Only evidence-supported analytics should be promoted to production.

## Institutional Roadmap

Institutional readiness would require:

- licensed data providers
- stronger legal review
- formal compliance boundaries
- observability
- rate limiting
- audit logs
- provider SLAs
- security review

This is future direction, not current production capability.
