## Purpose

The AI Opportunity Discovery Engine is a long-term product pillar for Market Pulse AI.

Its purpose is to discover high-quality investment opportunities before they become obvious to the broader market. It should not merely sort assets by recent performance or display cheap stocks. It should identify assets where evidence suggests an asymmetric opportunity may be forming.

The engine is a research and education layer. It does not predict winners, guarantee returns, or replace investor judgment.

## Product Philosophy

Market Pulse AI does not search for cheap stocks. It searches for asymmetric opportunities.

Price is only one signal. A low-priced asset without quality, liquidity, catalysts, evidence, or risk control is not an opportunity. A high-priced asset may still be attractive if growth, execution, market structure, and catalysts justify deeper research.

Every opportunity must be supported by evidence.

## Core Principle

The engine should answer:

- Why does this asset deserve attention now?
- What evidence supports the opportunity?
- What risks reduce the opportunity quality?
- What data is missing?
- What would invalidate the thesis?
- Why is confidence high, medium, or low?

The product should discover opportunities, not simply rank assets.

## Logical Architecture

The long-term opportunity discovery flow is:

Asset Universe
-> Eligibility Filter
-> Financial Engine
-> Technical Engine
-> Catalyst Engine
-> Narrative AI
-> Risk Penalty Engine
-> Multi-Bagger Similarity
-> Confidence Model
-> Opportunity Ranking
-> Explainable AI
-> Dashboard

Each stage must preserve evidence and disclose uncertainty.

## Signal Families

### Financial Signals

Financial signals may include revenue quality, margin direction, profitability, free cash flow, leverage, capital efficiency, valuation context, dilution risk, and balance sheet strength.

### Technical Signals

Technical signals may include trend, momentum, relative strength, volatility, support and resistance, volume behavior, market structure, and price behavior versus peers or benchmarks.

### Catalyst Signals

Catalyst signals may include new products, government policy, contracts, mergers and acquisitions, approvals, expansion, technology shifts, sector cycles, and supply chain changes.

### Narrative AI

Narrative AI should evaluate whether a company or asset is connected to an important emerging theme. Examples include AI infrastructure, semiconductors, energy transition, defense, healthcare innovation, liquidity cycles, or macro regime shifts.

Narrative alone is never enough. It must be supported by measurable evidence.

### Risk Penalty

Risk penalty exists to reduce opportunity quality when evidence shows dilution, weak liquidity, excessive leverage, accounting concerns, negative cash flow, delisting risk, pump-and-dump characteristics, or other severe warnings.

### Confidence Model

Confidence measures how reliable the available evidence is. It is not the same as expected return.

## Opportunity Ranking

Opportunity Ranking should combine opportunity strength, evidence quality, catalyst relevance, risk penalty, and confidence into an explainable prioritization model.

The ranking should help users decide what to research first. It should not imply that the highest-ranked asset must be bought.

## Explainability Standard

Every discovered opportunity must explain:

- What made it appear
- Which evidence categories contributed
- Which risks reduced the view
- What data is unavailable
- What would change the ranking
- Why confidence is not the same as upside

## What the Engine Must Not Do

The engine must not:

- Recommend based only on price
- Recommend based only on momentum
- Recommend based only on hype
- Fabricate catalysts
- Fabricate financial data
- Treat missing data as positive evidence
- Present similarities as predictions
- Encourage impulsive trading

## Future Expansion

Future opportunity discovery may include global opportunity intelligence, hidden gem detection, penny opportunity frameworks, multi-bagger similarity, macro opportunity maps, sector rotation intelligence, portfolio-aware discovery, and personalized research workflows.

The objective is intelligent discovery, not prediction.
