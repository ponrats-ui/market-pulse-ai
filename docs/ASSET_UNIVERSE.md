## Purpose

This document defines the long-term asset universe for Market Pulse AI.

The platform should support multiple asset classes while respecting that each asset class requires different evidence, analysis methods, terminology, and unavailable-state handling.

## Asset Universe

### Stocks

Public companies listed on supported exchanges. Stock analysis may include price, trend, fundamentals, valuation, sector, news, risk, and financial statements where available.

### ETF

Exchange-traded funds. ETF analysis should focus on holdings, index exposure, asset class, liquidity, fees where available, and market context.

### REIT

Real estate investment trusts. REIT analysis should consider yield, property exposure, interest-rate sensitivity, debt, occupancy, and sector conditions where available.

### Mutual Funds

Fund products that may require provider-specific data, holdings, performance, fees, risk profile, and suitability context where available.

### Indices

Market benchmarks and sector indices. Index analysis should focus on market direction, breadth, momentum, volatility, and macro context.

### Forex

Currency pairs and currency indices. Forex analysis should consider interest rates, inflation, central banks, current account context, risk appetite, and relative growth.

### Crypto

Digital assets. Crypto analysis should focus on price behavior, liquidity, volatility, network or ecosystem context where available, regulation, and risk appetite.

### Government Bonds

Sovereign debt and yield curves. Bond analysis should consider rates, inflation, policy expectations, credit risk, duration, and macro regime.

### Corporate Bonds

Company or sector credit instruments. Analysis should consider credit quality, spreads, rates, issuer strength, liquidity, and default risk where data exists.

### Precious Metals

Gold, silver, platinum, and related instruments. Analysis should consider dollar movement, real yields, inflation, safe-haven demand, and industrial demand where applicable.

### Energy

Oil, natural gas, and energy-related instruments. Analysis should consider supply, demand, inventories, geopolitics, OPEC, weather, and currency impact.

### Agriculture

Agricultural commodities and related instruments. Analysis should consider weather, supply chains, inventories, seasonality, currencies, and global demand.

### Interest Rates

Policy rates, yields, and curve indicators. These are central to valuation, liquidity, currency, and macro analysis.

### Economic Indicators

Inflation, employment, GDP, PMI, trade, central bank data, and liquidity indicators. These help explain market context.

### Alternative Assets

Future asset categories may include private markets, infrastructure, commodities baskets, volatility instruments, and thematic exposures where reliable data exists.

## Asset-Class Principles

- Use asset-appropriate analysis.
- Do not force stock-style analysis onto non-stock assets.
- Show unavailable financial statements when they do not apply.
- Explain the relevant drivers for each asset class.
- Attribute data sources clearly.

## Analysis Boundaries

Market Pulse AI should never fabricate asset coverage. Unsupported assets should be shown clearly as unsupported or unavailable.

## Expansion Standard

A new asset class is ready only when the product can explain its data, source, limitations, and analysis method clearly.
