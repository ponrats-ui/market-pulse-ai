## Purpose

The Catalyst Engine is a future framework for evaluating events, changes, and themes that may alter an asset's future outlook.

Its purpose is to identify why market attention could shift, not to fabricate events or predict exact timing.

## Catalyst Philosophy

A catalyst is useful only when it is supported by evidence.

Market Pulse AI should distinguish between:

- Confirmed catalysts
- Probable catalysts
- Potential catalysts
- Rumored or unsupported narratives
- No catalyst identified

Unsupported hype should not increase opportunity quality.

## Catalyst Categories

### New Products

Product launches, platform upgrades, new services, or technology releases that may change growth expectations.

### Government Policy

Policy shifts, subsidies, regulation, infrastructure programs, defense spending, energy policy, or industrial strategy.

### Contracts

Major customer wins, government contracts, supplier agreements, or backlog growth.

### Mergers and Acquisitions

Acquisition, divestiture, merger, consolidation, or strategic investment activity.

### Regulatory Approvals

FDA approvals, clinical milestones, permits, licenses, or other regulatory events.

### Expansion

New geographies, capacity expansion, distribution expansion, or new market entry.

### Technology Shifts

Structural technology transitions such as AI, semiconductors, cloud, automation, cybersecurity, robotics, and data centers.

### Industry Cycles

Supply-demand cycles, inventory cycles, commodity cycles, rate cycles, credit cycles, and sector rotations.

### Supply Chain

Capacity constraints, sourcing changes, bottlenecks, supplier risk, or demand shifts across the value chain.

## Catalyst Quality

Catalysts should be evaluated by:

- Evidence strength
- Timing visibility
- Business relevance
- Market awareness
- Potential magnitude
- Execution dependency
- Risk of disappointment

## Catalyst Score

The Catalyst Score should summarize the quality and relevance of potential catalysts.

It should not hardcode formulas in documentation. The score exists to help users understand whether the asset has identifiable reasons for future re-rating or attention.

## Evidence Requirements

The engine must identify the source and quality of catalyst evidence.

If catalyst evidence is unavailable, the product should say so clearly.

## Limitations

Catalysts can fail, arrive late, be priced in, or produce smaller effects than expected.

The Catalyst Engine supports research. It does not guarantee timing or outcome.
