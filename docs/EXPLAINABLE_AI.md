## Purpose

Explainable AI is the foundation of Market Pulse AI.

The product should help users understand financial information, not simply receive outputs. Every major AI response should make the reasoning visible enough for a user to evaluate it.

## Explainable AI Standard

AI outputs should be:

- Clear
- Evidence-based
- Cautious
- Structured
- Transparent about missing data
- Honest about uncertainty
- Educational rather than manipulative

## Core Questions

Market Pulse AI should help answer five questions.

### Why

Why does the system hold this view? What evidence supports it?

### How

How was the conclusion formed at a conceptual level? Which categories of evidence contributed?

### What

What does the output mean for the user's research process?

### What If

What conditions could change the conclusion? What risks could invalidate the current view?

### When

When should the user revisit the analysis? Which new data or events should be monitored?

## Recommendation Standard

Every recommendation should be explainable.

A responsible recommendation should include:

- Concise view
- Supporting evidence
- Positive factors
- Negative factors
- Risk factors
- Confidence level
- Missing data
- Conditions that could change the view
- Educational disclaimer

## Opportunity Discovery Explainability

Every discovered opportunity must explain why the asset appears.

The AI must never recommend or highlight an asset based only on price, momentum, or hype.

Opportunity discovery explanations should include:

- Why the asset entered the opportunity set
- Which financial signals contributed
- Which technical signals contributed
- Which catalyst signals contributed
- Which narrative signals contributed
- Which risks reduced the opportunity quality
- Whether similarity to historical high-growth companies is present
- Confidence level and why it differs from opportunity strength
- Missing data
- Conditions that would invalidate the view

If the system cannot explain why an asset appears, the asset should not be presented as a high-quality opportunity.

## User Understanding

Users should understand every score and recommendation well enough to challenge it.

The product should not ask users to trust unexplained outputs. It should teach users how to interpret the evidence.

## Trust Boundaries

Explainable AI does not mean exposing proprietary implementation details. It means showing enough reasoning, evidence categories, limitations, and uncertainty for the user to understand the product's output.
