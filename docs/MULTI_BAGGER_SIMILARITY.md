## Purpose

Multi-Bagger Similarity is a future framework for comparing current companies against historical high-growth companies.

The engine does not predict future winners. It identifies companies that share measurable characteristics with companies that historically created exceptional long-term returns.

## Philosophy

Similarity is a research signal, not a forecast.

Market Pulse AI should use historical patterns to help users ask better questions:

- Is this company showing early signs of durable growth?
- Is the market opportunity expanding?
- Is execution improving?
- Is the business model becoming more efficient?
- Are risks being ignored by the market?

## Historical Examples

Potential historical reference groups may include:

- NVIDIA
- AMD
- Netflix
- Monster Beverage
- Tesla
- Shopify

These examples should be used as pattern references, not as promises that new companies will repeat the same outcome.

## Similarity Dimensions

### Revenue Acceleration

Measures whether revenue growth is improving, sustained, or decelerating.

### Margin Expansion

Evaluates whether the business is gaining operating leverage or improving profitability quality.

### Industry Tailwind

Assesses whether the company benefits from a large structural market trend.

### Market Creation

Looks for companies creating or expanding a category rather than only competing in a mature market.

### Capital Efficiency

Reviews whether growth is being achieved with disciplined capital use.

### Founder Ownership

Considers whether founder or insider ownership aligns long-term incentives where data is available.

### Institutional Accumulation

Evaluates whether sophisticated capital appears to be building exposure, subject to data availability and reporting delays.

### Innovation

Reviews product, technology, distribution, or business-model innovation.

### Execution

Assesses evidence that management is delivering against growth, margins, product launches, and strategic milestones.

### Competitive Moat

Evaluates whether the company has durable advantages such as network effects, brand, scale, switching costs, intellectual property, data, or distribution.

## Interpretation

A high similarity view means the asset shares research-worthy characteristics with historical multi-bagger patterns.

It does not mean the asset will become a multi-bagger.

## What Similarity Does Not Mean

Similarity does not mean:

- Guaranteed upside
- Same future path
- Same valuation outcome
- Same risk profile
- Same market environment

Historical analogies must be handled carefully because markets, rates, competition, regulation, and capital availability change.

## Explainability Requirements

Every similarity output must show:

- Which historical traits are present
- Which traits are absent
- Which data is missing
- Which risks weaken the analogy
- Why confidence is high, medium, or low
- What future evidence would confirm or invalidate the comparison
