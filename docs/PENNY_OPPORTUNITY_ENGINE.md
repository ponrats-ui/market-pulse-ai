## Purpose

The Penny Opportunity Engine is a future research framework for identifying low-priced, smaller-cap, or early-stage assets that may deserve deeper investigation.

Penny stock is a category, not the investment thesis.

The engine must distinguish between an asset that is merely cheap and an asset that may have evidence of asymmetric opportunity.

## Founder Philosophy

Market Pulse AI does not search for cheap stocks. It searches for asymmetric opportunities.

A low share price can reflect early opportunity, but it can also reflect poor fundamentals, dilution, weak liquidity, delisting risk, or market distrust. The engine must treat price as a filter, not a conclusion.

## Universe Filter

The future engine may use an eligibility layer to classify assets by:

- Price category
- Exchange
- Market capitalization
- Liquidity
- Average spread
- Trading history
- Data availability
- Corporate status
- Provider coverage

The filter should never imply that all low-priced assets are attractive.

## Evaluation Framework

### Price Filter

Identifies assets in low-price or early-stage categories. This is only an entry point.

### Liquidity

Assesses whether the asset trades with enough volume and acceptable spread to support reasonable market participation.

### Market Cap

Provides size context. Smaller market cap may create upside potential but often increases volatility and financing risk.

### Fundamental Quality

Reviews revenue trend, margin direction, cash flow, debt, dilution, profitability path, and balance sheet resilience where available.

### Technical Strength

Reviews trend, momentum, relative strength, volatility, volume behavior, and market structure.

### Catalyst

Identifies real potential catalysts such as contracts, products, approvals, policy shifts, sector demand, or industry cycles.

### Narrative

Evaluates whether the asset is connected to a credible long-term theme. Narrative must be evidence-supported.

### Risk

Applies negative adjustments for severe risks including dilution, going concern warnings, delisting risk, reverse splits, negative cash flow, and extremely low liquidity.

### Confidence

Measures evidence quality and completeness. Low confidence should reduce the strength of any opportunity view.

### Final Ranking

Ranks assets by asymmetric opportunity quality, not cheapness.

## Penny Opportunity Score

The Penny Opportunity Score should summarize whether a low-priced asset deserves research attention.

It should conceptually reflect:

- Eligibility quality
- Liquidity quality
- Financial quality
- Technical quality
- Catalyst strength
- Narrative credibility
- Risk penalty
- Evidence confidence

It should not define fixed implementation constants in documentation.

## Risk Discipline

Penny opportunities must be handled with extra caution.

The engine should highlight:

- Speculation risk
- Liquidity risk
- Financing and dilution risk
- Promotion risk
- Volatility risk
- Incomplete data
- Regulatory and exchange risk

## Explainability Requirements

Every penny opportunity view must explain:

- Why the asset entered the candidate set
- What evidence supports deeper research
- What risks are severe
- What evidence is missing
- Why confidence is limited or strong
- Why price alone is insufficient

## Limitations

The Penny Opportunity Engine is not a penny stock recommendation engine. It is a disciplined discovery framework for education and research.

Users remain responsible for their own investment decisions.
