## 1. Executive Summary

Market Pulse AI is an Explainable AI Investment Intelligence Platform. It exists to help investors make better informed decisions through transparent analysis, real market data where available, clear unavailable states where data is missing, and cautious AI-assisted interpretation.

The Product Bible is the master reference for the project. It consolidates the product vision, founder principles, AI philosophy, governance model, documentation standards, roadmap, and long-term strategy into one executive-quality guide.

This document is suitable for developers, architects, founders, investors, enterprise customers, and future contributors. It should be read as the single entry point into the Market Pulse AI knowledge base.

## 2. Vision

Market Pulse AI aims to build the world's most trusted Explainable AI platform for global investment intelligence.

The product should become a professional, multi-asset, global research workspace that helps users understand markets, assets, risk, portfolio context, and cross-market relationships without relying on black-box outputs.

Primary reference: [VISION.md](VISION.md)

## 3. Mission

Market Pulse AI exists to help investors make better decisions through Explainable AI.

The platform is not a prediction engine. It is an investment intelligence platform. Its mission is to organize evidence, explain context, disclose uncertainty, and support disciplined decision-making.

Primary reference: [VISION.md](VISION.md)

## 4. Product Philosophy

AI should explain, educate, and increase understanding. It should never manipulate users, exaggerate certainty, or convert missing evidence into artificial confidence.

Users always make the final decision. Every score requires evidence. Transparency is more valuable than complexity. Simple is better than clever. Reliable is better than flashy.

Primary reference: [PRODUCT_PHILOSOPHY.md](PRODUCT_PHILOSOPHY.md)

## 5. Founder Principles

The Founder Constitution defines the permanent operating principles of Market Pulse AI:

- Production is the Source of Truth.
- Zero Mock Policy is non-negotiable.
- Explainability comes first.
- AI assists and humans decide.
- Every score must have evidence.
- Data must never be fabricated.
- Quality comes before quantity.
- One Prompt, One Commit, One Deploy, Founder Review, Approve.
- Architecture must be global-first.
- Maintainability is more important than shortcuts.
- User trust is the highest priority.
- Features must solve real user problems.

Primary reference: [FOUNDER_PRINCIPLES.md](FOUNDER_PRINCIPLES.md)

## 6. Explainable AI

Explainable AI is the foundation of Market Pulse AI. Every major AI response should make the reasoning visible enough for a user to evaluate it.

AI should answer:

- Why does this view exist?
- How was the conclusion formed conceptually?
- What does it mean for the user's research?
- What if conditions change?
- When should the user revisit the analysis?

Primary reference: [EXPLAINABLE_AI.md](EXPLAINABLE_AI.md)

## 7. Zero Mock Policy

Production must never display fabricated market data, charts, scores, news, financial values, indicators, or recommendations as real.

If data is unavailable, the product must show an explicit unavailable state with an explanation when possible. An honest empty state is more trustworthy than fake completeness.

Primary reference: [ZERO_MOCK_POLICY.md](ZERO_MOCK_POLICY.md)

## 8. AI Architecture

The logical AI architecture transforms market evidence into explainable investment intelligence:

Market Data -> Validation -> Normalization -> Feature Engineering -> Opportunity Score -> Market Context -> Market Adjusted Score -> Risk Analysis -> Chief Investment AI -> Investment Committee -> Recommendation -> Explanation

Each stage has a distinct responsibility. The architecture should preserve evidence, disclose uncertainty, and prevent unsupported conclusions.

Primary reference: [AI_ARCHITECTURE.md](AI_ARCHITECTURE.md)

## 9. Scoring Framework

Scores explain the market. They do not predict the future.

The scoring framework defines Opportunity Score, Market Score, Market Adjusted Score, Risk Score, and Confidence Score. Each score must have a purpose, meaning, interpretation standard, and evidence model.

Primary reference: [SCORING_FRAMEWORK.md](SCORING_FRAMEWORK.md)

## 10. Global Markets Strategy

Market Pulse AI is global-first. Long-term coverage should include major markets across North America, Europe, Asia, the Middle East, South America, and Africa.

Expansion must be disciplined. New market coverage should be added only when data quality, provider attribution, symbol normalization, and unavailable-state handling can be trusted.

Primary reference: [GLOBAL_MARKETS_STRATEGY.md](GLOBAL_MARKETS_STRATEGY.md)

## 11. Asset Universe

The long-term asset universe includes stocks, ETFs, REITs, mutual funds, indices, forex, crypto, government bonds, corporate bonds, precious metals, energy, agriculture, interest rates, economic indicators, and alternative assets.

Each asset class requires appropriate analysis. Stock-style financial statement analysis should not be forced onto assets where it does not apply.

Primary reference: [ASSET_UNIVERSE.md](ASSET_UNIVERSE.md)

## 12. Cross-Market Intelligence

Market Pulse AI should eventually understand relationships between markets instead of analyzing assets independently.

Examples include:

- PTT connected to oil, USDTHB, energy indices, and OPEC.
- AOT connected to tourism, oil, airlines, USDTHB, and airport traffic.
- NVDA connected to NASDAQ, semiconductor indices, yields, the dollar, AI, and data centers.
- Gold connected to the dollar, real yields, inflation, and the Federal Reserve.
- Bitcoin connected to liquidity, risk appetite, the dollar, and Nasdaq.

Primary reference: [GLOBAL_MARKETS_STRATEGY.md](GLOBAL_MARKETS_STRATEGY.md)

## 13. Product Positioning

Market Pulse AI is positioned as an explainable intelligence layer between raw financial data and investor decisions.

It is not a trading bot, guaranteed signal generator, or black-box recommendation system. Its advantage is trust through explainability.

Primary reference: [PRODUCT_POSITIONING.md](PRODUCT_POSITIONING.md)

## 14. Design Principles

The design philosophy is professional, readable, calm, and evidence-centered.

Core design standards include:

- Explain before recommending.
- Readable before beautiful.
- Simple before complicated.
- Evidence before confidence.
- Mobile-first and desktop professional.
- Accessible by default.
- Internationalized for Thai and English.
- Consistent interaction patterns.
- Avoid unnecessary visual complexity.

Primary reference: [DESIGN_PRINCIPLES.md](DESIGN_PRINCIPLES.md)

## 15. Data Governance

Data governance protects trust. Data must be validated, normalized, attributed, monitored, and presented honestly.

The product must distinguish real provider data, unavailable data, stale data, partial data, and derived analysis. Fallbacks may improve resilience, but they must never fabricate data.

Primary reference: [DATA_GOVERNANCE.md](DATA_GOVERNANCE.md)

## 16. AI Ethics

AI ethics in Market Pulse AI requires transparency, fairness, bias reduction, explainability, no manipulation, no emotional persuasion, evidence-based output, responsible AI, user autonomy, and human decision authority.

AI should remain calm, disciplined, and educational.

Primary reference: [AI_ETHICS.md](AI_ETHICS.md)

## 17. Risk Management

Risk communication must be visible, understandable, and connected to evidence.

The product should explain market uncertainty, confidence levels, probability versus certainty, warnings, limitations, refusal boundaries, and user responsibility.

Primary reference: [RISK_MANAGEMENT.md](RISK_MANAGEMENT.md)

## 18. Security Principles

Security principles define the long-term posture for authentication, authorization, least privilege, secret management, rate limiting, logging, audit trails, input validation, and future enterprise security.

Secrets must not be committed. Sensitive capabilities should be protected by appropriate authorization and secure configuration.

Primary reference: [SECURITY_PRINCIPLES.md](SECURITY_PRINCIPLES.md)

## 19. Non-Functional Requirements

Market Pulse AI should meet long-term quality expectations for performance, reliability, availability, scalability, maintainability, accessibility, internationalization, security, privacy, observability, logging, monitoring, testing, response times, and future enterprise readiness.

Primary reference: [NON_FUNCTIONAL_REQUIREMENTS.md](NON_FUNCTIONAL_REQUIREMENTS.md)

## 20. Competitive Position

Market Pulse AI operates near established tools such as Bloomberg Terminal, TradingView, Yahoo Finance, Google Finance, Seeking Alpha, Simply Wall St, and Morningstar.

Its competitive position is not based on being the largest data terminal. It is based on transparent, explainable, multi-factor investment intelligence that helps users understand evidence and risk.

Primary reference: [COMPETITIVE_POSITION.md](COMPETITIVE_POSITION.md)

## 26. Documentation Governance

Documentation is part of the product, not an afterthought.

The following governance policy is permanent:

- Every architectural change must update documentation.
- Every roadmap change must update [ROADMAP.md](ROADMAP.md).
- Every scoring change must update [SCORING_FRAMEWORK.md](SCORING_FRAMEWORK.md).
- Every AI philosophy change must update [EXPLAINABLE_AI.md](EXPLAINABLE_AI.md).
- Every global market expansion must update [GLOBAL_MARKETS_STRATEGY.md](GLOBAL_MARKETS_STRATEGY.md).
- Every new supported asset class must update [ASSET_UNIVERSE.md](ASSET_UNIVERSE.md).
- Every vision change must update [VISION.md](VISION.md) and [CHANGELOG_VISION.md](CHANGELOG_VISION.md).
- Documentation is part of the Definition of Done.

Future changes should update existing documents rather than creating unnecessary new documentation.

## 27. Definition of Done

A change is not complete unless the relevant documentation remains accurate.

For future work, Definition of Done includes:

- Code or documentation change is scoped and reviewable.
- No unrelated changes are mixed into the commit.
- Production-facing behavior is verified in production when applicable.
- Documentation is updated for architecture, roadmap, scoring, AI philosophy, market expansion, asset class support, or vision changes.
- Known limitations are documented honestly.
- Founder review can evaluate the change without hidden assumptions.

After this sprint, the repository contains a complete product knowledge base. Future work should focus on product development and maintain these foundational documents instead of creating unnecessary parallel documentation.
