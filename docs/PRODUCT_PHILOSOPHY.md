## Purpose

This document defines how Market Pulse AI should think about product decisions. It protects the product from becoming flashy, manipulative, overcomplicated, or untrustworthy.

## AI Should Explain

AI should explain.

AI should educate.

AI should increase understanding.

AI should help the user see the structure of a situation: facts, interpretation, risks, confidence, missing data, and possible next questions.

AI should never hide uncertainty behind confident language.

## Education Before Persuasion

Market Pulse AI should teach users how to read market information. The product should not pressure users toward action.

A good output helps the user understand:

- What happened
- Why it may matter
- What evidence supports the view
- What evidence is missing
- What risks should be monitored
- What could change the conclusion

## User Decision Authority

Users always make the final decision.

Market Pulse AI may provide educational observations, cautious actions, risk warnings, and transparent analysis. It must not imply that the AI knows the future or that the user should blindly follow a recommendation.

## Evidence for Every Score

Every score requires evidence.

If a score cannot be explained, it should not be trusted. The product should show what the score is intended to summarize and what the score does not mean.

Scores should support judgment. They should not replace judgment.

## Transparency Over Complexity

Transparency is more valuable than complexity.

A simple, explainable model is better than a complex black box when users cannot understand the reasoning. The product may become more sophisticated over time, but complexity must earn its place through clarity, reliability, and user value.

## Simple, Reliable, Useful

Simple is better than clever.

Reliable is better than flashy.

Useful is better than impressive.

The product should prioritize workflows that investors can use repeatedly without confusion, mistrust, or unnecessary friction.

## Product Boundaries

Market Pulse AI should never manipulate users, fabricate confidence, or convert missing data into artificial certainty.

The product should remain calm, professional, educational, and transparent even when markets are volatile.
