## Purpose

Risk management documentation defines how Market Pulse AI should communicate uncertainty and downside risk.

## Investment Risk Communication

Risk should be visible, understandable, and connected to evidence.

The product should explain risk factors such as volatility, downside, liquidity, concentration, event risk, provider limitations, stale data, and missing evidence.

## Market Uncertainty

Markets are uncertain. Market Pulse AI should avoid language that implies certainty, guaranteed outcomes, or inevitable returns.

Uncertainty should be framed calmly and professionally.

## Confidence Levels

Confidence should reflect evidence quality, data completeness, consistency, and freshness.

Low confidence should weaken recommendations and increase caution.

## Probability vs Certainty

The product should prefer probability over prediction.

Probabilistic language helps users understand uncertainty. Certainty language can mislead users into overconfidence.

## When AI Should Refuse Recommendations

AI should refuse or soften recommendations when:

- Data is missing or unreliable
- The asset is unsupported
- Evidence conflicts strongly
- The question asks for guaranteed profit
- The request asks for personalized regulated advice beyond product scope
- The request depends on unknown personal financial circumstances

## Warning Presentation

Warnings should be visible without being alarmist.

Important risk warnings should be concise, specific, and tied to evidence. The interface should avoid burying risk in long paragraphs.

## Limitations

Market Pulse AI cannot guarantee returns, predict market moves with certainty, know every user's personal situation, or eliminate investment risk.

Provider delays, market closures, data outages, and incomplete coverage may affect analysis.

## User Responsibility

Users remain responsible for their decisions.

Market Pulse AI provides educational analysis and decision support, not financial advice or trade execution.
