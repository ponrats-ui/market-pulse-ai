## Purpose

The Risk Penalty Engine is a future framework for reducing opportunity quality when material downside risks are present.

It exists because attractive narratives and strong momentum can hide serious risks.

## Why Risk Penalty Exists

Opportunity discovery must be disciplined. A candidate should not rank highly if its risks are severe, unexplained, or unsupported by adequate evidence.

Risk penalty protects the product from presenting fragile, speculative, or low-quality assets as high-quality opportunities.

## Risk Penalty Categories

### Dilution

Share issuance, convertibles, warrants, or financing structures that may reduce shareholder value.

### Reverse Split

Reverse splits may indicate listing pressure or sustained weakness. They require context and should not be treated as automatically positive.

### Going Concern

Auditor or company disclosures indicating material uncertainty about continued operations.

### Excessive Debt

Debt load, refinancing pressure, interest burden, or covenant risk.

### Negative Cash Flow

Persistent cash burn, poor operating cash generation, or dependence on external financing.

### Accounting Warning

Restatements, auditor changes, internal-control weaknesses, delayed filings, or unusual accounting signals.

### Delisting Risk

Exchange compliance issues, price requirements, reporting delays, or market-cap thresholds.

### Pump-and-Dump Characteristics

Unusual promotional activity, unsupported hype, extreme volume spikes without evidence, or sharp moves in illiquid assets.

### Extremely Low Liquidity

Low volume, wide spreads, poor price discovery, and high execution risk.

### High Spread

Wide bid-ask spreads that create hidden transaction costs and reduce practical investability.

## Severity

Risk penalties should consider severity, evidence quality, persistence, and whether the risk is already reflected in the opportunity view.

Documentation should not define fixed constants. The concept is that more severe and better-supported risks should reduce opportunity quality more strongly.

## Relationship to Opportunity Score

Risk Penalty is not the same as Risk Score.

Risk Score describes risk level. Risk Penalty adjusts opportunity ranking when risk weakens the case.

An asset can have high opportunity signals and still receive a lower final ranking because the risk penalty is severe.

## Explainability Requirements

Every risk penalty should explain:

- Which risk was detected
- Why it matters
- What evidence supports it
- How severe it appears conceptually
- What data is missing
- What would reduce or increase the penalty
