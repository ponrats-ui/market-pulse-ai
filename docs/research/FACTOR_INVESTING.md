# Factor Investing

## Purpose

Factor investing studies recurring characteristics that may explain differences in asset returns over time.

Market Pulse AI uses factor thinking as a conceptual foundation for evidence-based analysis, not as a promise that any factor will work in every market.

## Core Concepts

Common factor families include:

- Value
- Quality
- Momentum
- Size
- Low volatility
- Profitability
- Investment discipline
- Yield

Each factor can behave differently across markets, cycles, asset classes, and time horizons.

## Product Relevance

Factor investing supports Market Pulse AI by encouraging the system to evaluate multiple dimensions instead of relying on a single signal.

Opportunity discovery should ask whether an asset has quality evidence, momentum evidence, valuation context, risk control, market support, and confidence in available data.

## Limitations

Factors can underperform for long periods. Crowding, regime changes, transaction costs, liquidity, and data quality can weaken factor usefulness.

Market Pulse AI should explain factor evidence cautiously and never treat factor exposure as a guaranteed outcome.
