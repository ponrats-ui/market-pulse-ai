# Momentum Investing

## Purpose

Momentum investing studies the tendency for assets with strong recent relative performance to continue attracting attention for some period.

Momentum is useful because markets often adjust gradually to new information.

## Common Momentum Signals

- Price trend
- Relative strength
- Breakouts
- Volume confirmation
- Sector leadership
- New highs
- Trend persistence

## Product Relevance

Market Pulse AI can use momentum to identify assets that deserve attention, especially when momentum aligns with quality, catalysts, and market context.

Momentum should not stand alone. Strong price movement without evidence can reflect hype, low liquidity, or speculative excess.

## Limitations

Momentum can reverse sharply. It can become crowded. It can be misleading in illiquid assets.

The AI must never recommend based only on momentum.
