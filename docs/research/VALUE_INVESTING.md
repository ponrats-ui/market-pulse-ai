# Value Investing

## Purpose

Value investing studies assets that may trade below a reasonable estimate of intrinsic worth.

The concept is not simply buying low-priced assets. It is evaluating whether price is attractive relative to quality, risk, cash flow, assets, and future prospects.

## Common Value Signals

- Low valuation multiples
- Strong free cash flow yield
- Asset backing
- Earnings normalization
- Temporary pessimism
- Margin of safety
- Balance sheet strength

## Product Relevance

Market Pulse AI should distinguish cheap from undervalued.

An asset may be cheap because it is risky, deteriorating, illiquid, or structurally challenged. The product should explain why valuation appears attractive or why it may be a value trap.

## Limitations

Value signals can remain out of favor. Accounting data may lag. Low multiples can reflect real decline.

Value analysis must include catalysts, quality, market context, and risk penalty.
